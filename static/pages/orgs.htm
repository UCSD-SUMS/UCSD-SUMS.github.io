Student and Alumni Organizations

UCSD Tritons for Mathematics

The UCSD Tritons for Mathematics is an alumni organization. Membership is open to graduates of the UCSD Department of Mathematics. The mission of this alumni organization is to support students, research, and programs in mathematics, as well as provide opportunities for social, professional, and business contacts among membership. The organization is a great way to keep in touch with friends, make new ones, and find out what Mathematics alumni are doing.

 

Mu Alpha Theta (The Math Club)

The UCSD Math Club provides opportunities for UCSD students to find out about internships and career opportunities. The club organizes social and professional networking events, community service projects and invites speakers to its colloquia. All Mathematics majors are encouraged to participate in club activities early in their undergraduate academic careers.

 

Career Opportunites

The Mathematics Department maintains several folders on career opportunities with a degree in mathematics. Please feel free to stop by and take a look at our information. In addition, we have a folder containing information on internship opportunities. We're always receiving new materials and deadlines vary so we advise reading through the folders regularly. This is a wonderful resource and a good starting point for your search. Internship information is also available through the Academic Internship Program (AIP), which maintains listing of internships relevant to Mathematics for the summer and academic year.

The college graduate with a bachelor's degree in mathematics can qualify for a broad range of highly paid positions in business, industry, government, and teaching. Companies in the computer and communications industries employ many mathematicians, as do oil companies, banks, insurance companies, and consulting firms.

Almost every bureau and branch of the federal government employs mathematicians in some capacity. Mathematicians, statisticians, operations researchers, and actuaries work in the Department of Health and Human Services, the General Accounting Office, the Office of Management and Budget, and the National Institute of Standards. The Department of Energy, the Department of Defense, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, and the National Security Agency also employ many mathematicians.
