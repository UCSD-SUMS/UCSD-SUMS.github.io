Majors in Math
UCSD Department of Mathematics

 

Pure Mathematics

The Pure Mathematics major is designed for students who are interested in the theory of mathematics. Pure mathematics majors often pursue Master's degrees and Ph.D.'s in Mathematics in order to prepare for a career in research or university level teaching.

 

Applied Mathematics

Applied Mathematicians are interested in the classical relationships between mathematics, the physical sciences, and engineering. They often seek employment in industry, utilizing their skills to solve engineering and computer related problems.

 

Applied Mathematics - Scientific Programming

The Mathematics-Scientific Programming major is designed for individuals who are interested in mathematical theory and the related applications of computing. These majors often seek employment in areas similar to the Applied Mathematicians.

 

Mathematics - Secondary Education

Math-Secondary Education is a major designed for students who have a substantial interest in teaching mathematics at the secondary level. The senior year includes a paid internship.

 

Mathematics - Computer Science

The Mathematics-Computer Science major trains mathematically oriented computer scientists who have specialized knowledge in the mathematical aspects and foundations of computer science or in the computer applications of mathematics.

 

Mathematics - Applied Science

The Mathematics-Applied Science major is intended for those students who are interested in mathematics, but who also have a substantial interest in the applications of mathematics to other areas. Students majoring in Mathematics/Applied Science often pursue careers in actuarial science, management consulting, financial planning, accounting, engineering, medical and life sciences or operations research.

Students who major in Mathematics-Applied Science may pursue one of the following plans, for example, or others which can be custom designed: 

  1. Chemistry and Biochemistry
  2. Medical and Life Sciences
  3. Cognitive Science and Psychology
  4. Applied Mechanics & Engineering
  5. Biology and Bioengineering
  6. Electrical and Computer Engineering

     

Mathematics-Economics

The Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics-Economics is particularly appropriate preparation for graduate study in business and economics because the top-ranked graduate programs require strong mathematical qualifications for admission. It gives an excellent background to students applying to finance programs in graduate schools of management. It also provides a solid foundation for entry level employment in a number of areas of business, finance & management. Majors are encouraged to undertake an undergraduate internship.
