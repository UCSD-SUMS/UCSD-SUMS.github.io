January's Brainteaser:
solved by Mike Skirvin solution
Let (X,d) be a compact metric space and f:X -> X an isometry. Recall that this means d(f(x),f(y)) = d(x,y) for every x,y in X. It's easy to see that f is continuous and injective. Show that f is bijective.


December's Brainteaser:
solved by Mike Skirvin solution
Let G be a finite monoid with e denoting the identity element of G. Suppose p,q are in G such that pq = e. Show that qp = e. Also construct an example of infinite monoid G with elements p,q such that pq = e but qp != e.


November's Brainteaser:
solved by Michael Viscardi solution
Suppose the series a_1 + sqrt(2)*a_2 + sqrt(3)*a_3 + ... + sqrt(n)*a_n + ... converges (a_i are complex numbers). Show that a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + ... converges.


October's Brainteaser:
solved by Wolfgang Kais solution
For positive integer n,
1 + 2 + 3 + ... + n = n(n+1)/2
1^2 + 2^2 + ... + n^2 = n(n+1) (2n+1)/6
etc..
part (a) Show that given any positive integer p, there exists a unique polynomial S(x,p) with rational coefficients with indeterminate x such that S(n,p) = 1^p + 2^p + ... + n^p for all evaluations at positive integer n.

part (b) Show that if p > 0 is even, then 0, -1, and -1/2 are roots of the polynomial S(x,p). If p > 1 is odd, then 0 and -1 are double roots of S(x,p). i.e., if p > 0 is even then x(x+1)(2x+1) is a factor of S(x,p) and if p > 1 is odd then (x(x+1))^2 is a factor of S(x,p).

You need to solve both parts.
