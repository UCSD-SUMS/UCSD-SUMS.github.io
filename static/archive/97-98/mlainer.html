Maxim Lainer

Maxim Lainer received a bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics from John Muir College at UCSD in 1992 and a master's degree in Statistics from UC Berkeley in 1995. Since the fall of 1995, Maxim has been employed as a systems engineer at Gencorp Aerojet's Azusa, CA site.

Aerojet is an aerospace company of about 3000 employees, with core competencies in rocket propulsion systems, remote sensing, and custom chemicals. Gencorp, the parent company, is a diversified corporation of about 10,000 employees. Aerojet's Azusa site focuses on remote sensing systems such as space-based early warning systems used for missile detection, space-based systems for environmental and climatological data gathering, and smart munitions.

During his time at Aerojet, Maxim has contributed to algorithm development efforts in areas such as tracking, classification/discriminant analysis, multisensor data fusion, and parameter estimation. He has also provided mathematical and statistical support for a variety of phenomenology studies and other projects.

If you are interested in learning more about Aerojet and/or career options for UCSD math majors, please feel free to contact Maxim. A wide range of company information can also be accessed at Aerojet's Web site (http://www.aerojet.com). US citizenship and the ability to obtain a security clearance are generally required for employment.
