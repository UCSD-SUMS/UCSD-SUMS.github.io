During my time at UCSD I worked in an engineering position at a company called Linkabit. They are still in business and owned by Titan right across from the Torrey Pines Golf Course. My time there was invaluable in preparing me for work after graduation. In fact, I was already an engineer when I graduated from UCSD. In the years since graduation in 1986/87 I have started new divisions using Department of Defense (DOD) technology transfer concepts, started my own company, retired and re-joined the workforce at what is basically a startup company where I am involved in all aspects of the business operations.

I began as a lab rat, and still feel comfortable in developing SW but I have been lucky enough to have kept my engineering roots and moved into the equally exciting aspects on the business side. At this time I am responsible for the development of projects in places such as Mexico City, Budapest and London.

I am interested in supporting the concept of providing insight into what opportunities the real world provides if a person is motivated and willing to think outside the box. I am proud to say that a person who supported me as an office assistant has just graduated from UCSD with her own engineering degree based on her willingness to take a chance. It took her over six years but she now is doing what I did at Titan, working as an engineer and thriving both personally and professionally.

Although my support for the Mathematics Department has been limited (or nonexistent) I feel that I may have an interesting story to tell and some real-world examples to bring to the table. And, with all the stuff that is going on here at Coded, there are opportunities for internships for the right type of person.
