The Problem:

Shooting Hoops

Joe, Ben, and Maggie have a free throw contest. Whoever makes the first free throw is the winner. Being the youngest, Joe, who only makes 40% of his baskets, gets the first shot. Ben, who makes 60% of his baskets, then shoots, followed by Maggie, who makes 70% of her baskets. If all three players miss, Joe shoots again and they continue until someone wins.

What is the probability that Joe wins the game?

What is the probability that Ben wins the game?

What is the probability that Maggie wins the game?

The Solution:

 

The sample space for the experiment described can be represented as

J
J B
J B M
J B M J
J B M J B
J B M J B M
J B M J B M J
...

where J stands for Joe, B stands for Ben, and M stands for Maggie, and each outcome listed shows the succession of free-throws shot terminating in the person who finally makes the shot.

 

Now the series of outcomes where Joe wins is

J
J B M J
J B M J B M J
...

 

The probability Joe makes the initial shot is given as 0.4
The probability that Joe misses a shot is 1-0.4 = 0.6,
The probability that Ben misses a shot is 1-0.6 = 0.4,
and the probability that Maggie misses a shot is 1-0.7 = 0.3

 

Thus, the first outcome in this series has a probability of

0.4

 

The second outcome has a probability of

(0.6)(0.4)(0.3)*0.4 = 0.072 * 0.4

 

The third outcome has a probability of

(0.6)(0.4)(0.3)(0.6)(0.4)(0.3)*0.4 = (0.072)^2 * 0.4

 

Similarly, the kth outcome consists of k-1 appearances of J B M where all three miss, followed by J making the basket, so the probability for the kth outcome is

((0.6)(0.4)(0.3))^(k-1) * 0.4 = (0.072)^(k-1) * 0.4

 

Thus, the sums of the probabilities of every outcome where Joe wins is

(0.4) + (0.4)(0.072) + (0.4)(0.072)^2 + ... + (0.4)(0.072)^(k-1) + ...

 

This is a geometric series where the factor is 0.072 < 1, so the sum is

(0.4)/(1 - 0.072)
= (0.4)/(0.928)

= 25/58

 

The sequence of outcomes where Ben wins is

J B
J B M J B
J B M J B M J B
...

 

The kth outcome this time has a probability of

(0.072)^(k-1) * (0.6)(0.6)

by the same reasoning as above, where the 0.6 * 0.6 is the combination of Joe missing his shot and Ben making his shot in the last round. Thus, the sum of the probabilities for outcomes where Ben wins is again a geometric series:

(0.6)(0.6) + (0.6)(0.6)(0.072) + (0.6)(0.6)(0.072)^2 + ... + (0.6)(0.6)(0.072)^(k-1) + ...
= (0.6)(0.6)/(1 - 0.072)

= (0.36)/(0.928)

= 90/232

 

Finally the sequence of outcomes where Maggie wins is

J B M
J B M J B M
J B M J B M J B M
...

 

The kth outcome this time has a probability of

(0.072)^(k-1) * (0.6)(0.4)(0.7)
= (0.072)^(k-1) * (0.168)

where the (0.6)(0.4)(0.7) comes from the final round where Joe and Ben miss their shots, and Maggie makes hers.

 

The sum of the probabilities for outcomes where Maggie wins is again a geometric series

(0.168) + (0.168)(0.072) + (0.168)(0.072)^2 + ... + (0.168) (0.072)^(k-1) + ...
= (0.168)/(1 - 0.072)

= (0.168)/(0.928)

= 21/116

 

In summary, the probability that Joe wins the game is 25/58 (~ 43%)

the probability that Ben wins the game is 90/232 (~ 39%)

and the probability that Maggie wins the game is 21/116 (~ 18%)
