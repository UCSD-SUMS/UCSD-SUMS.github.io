John V. Colletto

Degree: B.A. Applied Mathematics, Minor in Economics, 1989

Employer Name: Sybase, Inc.
Employer Description: Database Software
Job Title: Compensation Analyst

Job Description: I work within the Sales Operation department of Sybase. My job entails tracking revenue accruals and paying commissions to sales reps. Additionally, I am responsible for reporting revenue by district and sales rep performance on a monthly, quarterly, and annual basis.

Describe a typical day at work: It varies day to day. I mainly deal with issues involving deals booked by the reps. I work with the company's computer systems to find the deals and report back to the rep on its location. Occassionally, I am involved in special projects creating databases to store and report information, and program spreadsheets to automate the departments monthly processes.

Skills used: Lots of work with spreadsheets-Microsoft Excel. Reports are generated through Microsoft Access. You must be able to accurately communicate ideas and concepts to the sales representatives and managers.

How did you come to choose this job/a career in this field: Found the job through friends - I wanted to use my math background in a business setting. I found the programming environment was too boring for me. I wanted a job that interacted with people on a daily basis.

What you like most about your job: Working with individuals at all levels of the company -- a vice president one day, maybe a sales rep in Chicago the next.

What you like least about your job: During the quarter end, the hours are very long (12 midnight)

Why did you choose math as a major? I enjoy solving mathematical problems. Math provides a solid background for entering the business world.

Favorite Mathematician: Albert Einstein and/or Sir Isaac Newton (tie)

Advice/words of wisdom for undergrads: Mathematical Programming background is an excellent starting point/degree for entering the high tech industry. The saying "Its not what you know, but who you know" applies to finding a job. Sending resumes to companies will get you nowhere. You need to know someone within the company of interest that can speak on your behalf. Also, getting good grades is important but you need to be a well rounded individual with outside interests like sports or a social club. Employers want a person, not a book-worm. Communication skills are very important in the business world.
