Degree: Ph.D. Mathematical Statistics, 1981

Why did you choose math as a major? Math is always the center of science. In addition, statistics is closer to daily life than some of the pure math subjects.

Job Title: Director

Job Description: I have been through 3 jobs: AT&T Bell Labs, a pharmaceutical company, and an investment company. By far, Bell Labs has the highest appreciation of the technical skills. The pharmaceutical company needed lots of data processing and analytical skills in the biostatistics area. The investment company needed quick thinking rather than actual deep knowledge. (Most of the people do not have high degree in this field.) The most important thing for a statistician is to not to be afraid of numbers, a lot of numbers, as data processing is major part of the daily life. If you do not like to get your hands dirty, I suggest you either go into teaching or into some research institutes. From my experience, job satisfaction is far more important than money. A good boss is far more important than a good company. I changed my jobs primarily because of the boss not the company. Sometimes, when your boss asks you to do some work which you really do not like or conflicts with your personal goals, it is the time to change jobs. Remember, if you can keep updating yourself (and not make your life too easy), you can always find opportunities out there.
